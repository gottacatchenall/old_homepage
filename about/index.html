Hello!

I’m a master’s student in the department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at the University of Colorado at Boulder, working with Dr. Sam Flaxman.

I consider myself a theoretical ecologist, but my research involves topics from evolutionary genetics to stochastic processes. My undergrad background was primarily in math and computing, and as a result a major focus of my work is developing models of ecological systems guided by the principles of abstraction and generalizability. In general, I am interested in using developing models and predictive software tools for the conservation and management of protected species in the face of rapid anthropogenic change.

When I’m not doing math in some form, I enjoy hikes, espresso, and a good book.

For more information about current and future projects, see the research page.
